About Me

I am a PhD student in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Seoul National University, advised by Dr. Jinsoo Kim. I completed my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering at UNIST with a GPA of 4.20/4.30.

My research focuses on enhancing human mobility through advanced wearable robotics and biomechanical analysis. I am particularly interested in developing intelligent systems that can adapt to and improve human gait patterns, with applications ranging from rehabilitation to sports performance enhancement.

Research Interests

My research interests include lower-limb wearable robots, gait augmentation, and human-robot interaction. I am particularly focused on improving gait performance and generating optimal motion patterns to enhance human mobility, with long-term applications in sports and performance augmentation.

Wearable Robotics

Designing and developing lower-limb exoskeletons and soft wearable robots for gait assistance and augmentation

Biomechanics

Analyzing human gait patterns using motion capture systems and developing biomechanical models

Human-Robot Interaction

Creating intuitive control strategies and optimizing motion patterns for seamless human-robot collaboration

Gait Augmentation

Developing algorithms to enhance human mobility and performance in sports and daily activities
